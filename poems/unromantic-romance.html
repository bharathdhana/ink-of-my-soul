Unromantic Romance

After a long day,
I returned home,
Walked straight to my room
Lying lifeless on my bed,
Staring at the ceiling.

My mind echoes,
The unfading touch of nightmares,
Bittersweet moments
From the months spent with you.

No expectations,
Yet still—can’t believe.
Assumptions failed.
You chose someone else,
After making me believe
I was the one—
Gifted, chosen, admired.
In the end, I wasn’t.

What made me chase you, choose you?
When I was doing fine on my own?
Why did you appear in my view?
Why those windows, those stares?
Why those smiles, those waving hands?

You came like a star,
And disappeared
Like ashes in the wind.

You left me in a void;
A hollow space of hopeless hope.
An unromantic romance,
Distance,
Devouring me silently.

My emotions—my own.
Never your responsibility.
How? Yet somehow?
Your inconvenience, your guilt,
Became my burden.

I don’t want you back.
Stay where you are—
Left in an ocean of sharks,
Don’t bother wondering
How I make it to shore?

I chose peace.
I chose solitude.
I chose me—
Over you.
