A Stray Cat and the Last Meal

A fluffy, small,
Cute little four-legged soul,
Old, seeking warmth—
And something to fill a hungry belly.

Lost in the town,
Chased and beaten by shopkeepers,
Abandoned by the one it once loved.
Wandering the alley walls,
Craving affection, craving scraps.

It waits across the bustling restaurant,
In the heart of the city,
Hoping the waiters will toss away
The final bits of chopped fish.
Surrounded by barking street dogs,
It shivers,
Then flees to the town hall's edge.

Swollen legs, bruised face,
Folded ears, hunter's eyes—
Ready to dash, to dream,
To open wings and fly beyond.

At last, a final piece is thrown,
Clutched in its mouth,
It sprints, hides beneath a bush.

Eats the last meal,
Sleeps the last sleep—
Wakes up in heaven.
