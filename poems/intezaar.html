Intezaar

"Intezaar" –
The most beautiful word
I've ever heard.

Waiting for something,
A delicate, heart full thing.

A farmer waits for the sky
To pour down rain,
For a small seed
To grow into a beautiful tree,
For a bud to blossom
Into a glorious, colourful flower.

A father waits for his child
To enter this world,
Like decades passing,
As if waiting for a shrub
To bloom upon distant hills,
Or for another leap year to arrive.

As if waiting for a solar eclipse,
Or a child watching the clock,
Hoping for their parent’s return
From a long day at school.
Hoping for someone, somewhere,
To return home safe,
With miracles yet to unfold
In their own life.

Even gods wait for their goddesses –
As Radha waits for Krishna,
As Sita waits for Rama,
As Urmila waits for Laxman.

Waiting for loved ones,
Waiting for a heart to turn,
To accept the love offered.

"Intezaar" –
Not everyone can wait for you,
But if you find one who does,
Don’t let them go.
