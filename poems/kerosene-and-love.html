Kerosene and Love

A strange glance cast over me—
From afar,
She wanted me to saw her back.
For a moment, instead
I fell into
A glimpse of you and I.
Intended to care, love, linger, but—
All she want was—a look, a moment's attention.
Care,
Taken for granted.
Love,
Seen as baggage of burden, unwanted for the moment.
All I showed was pure, No libido,
But she craved more and more,
Like how it ended—
I was burning down in the fire
Kerosene all over me,
She stood in front of me,
She asked,
“Why won't you see me?”
My skin burned,
Aching to my heart,
Bones turning black.
Me, and my love for you,
Burned all away.
Still, you only craved attention,
Peak attention.
You lunatic.
What kind of animal are you?
