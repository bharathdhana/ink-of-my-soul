A Boy and a Coin

A poor mother gave her son a penny,
“Just enough”, she smiled, “for a treat, if any.”
He held it close, a treasure so bright,
A little golden star, his heart's delight.

Off to the shop, he skipped with glee,
But the coin slipped, rolled to a tree.
Into the grass, lost from the sight,
But nature stirred with magic that night.

A squirrel peeked out, quick and spry,
It nudged the coin with a curious eye.
Daisies leaned in, their petals glow,
Guiding the coin where the soft winds blow.

A fish in the brook flicked it high,
And a robin swooped down from the sky.
The boy caught the coin with joy anew,
A small bit of magic, a blessing so true.
