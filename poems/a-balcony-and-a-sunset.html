A Balcony and a Sunset

I woke after a long sleep,
with a hot cup of coffee in my hand—
standing in the balcony,
staring at the evening orange sunset.

My mind begins to fill with—
some strange thoughts.

Love, and the kind of relationships—
They aren't easy at all,
You have to work on them,
spend time and efforts.

Talking about relationships...
letting go holds dimensions of pain.
There is a death,
and also,
a morning sunrise in letting go.

Happiness—
You can find in different ways,
from different people,

Sometimes, it hides in:
giving gifts, receiving gifts,
showing and offering love,
receiving love, helping people,
caring, hugging, playful teasing,
spending for the ones you love.

And also—
Sometimes,
happiness flows out from the eyes:
inaudible, voiceless, silent tears—
unveiled feelings.

Horrible?
Life is strange,
Sometimes, too strange.
Holding on to the past
feels like yapping about an unseen ghost.

Life is strange,
because strangers are strange.
Strangers become friends,
The most trusted ones, partners...
And sometimes lovers.

Seeking the red string—
Yet never found it,
So I decided to chase the sun and stars,
In the mid skies of day and night.

Listening to those thoughts,
my cup becomes empty.
As so—
ready to refill.
