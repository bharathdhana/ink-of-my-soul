Ocean Colors

Standing down on my two feet,
On the wet sand,
Orange and blue skies above,
Cold breeze, a warm space.
Sparkling waves and blue tides,
A fierce battle—waves clash,
Like heart and brain.
I saw myself, standing all alone,
For a quiet bit of a moment—
No grudges. No judges.
Shore waves touch my feet,
Washing away sins and ego.
My body turns light—like a cloud,
Levitating in a state of emotion.
No tears. No jinx.
Wishing to be me for some time,
Waiting for myself—to reclaim me,
To hold me, to choose me.
The world can turn its back,
Yet I stand again—for myself,
For who I am—
Not for who they want me to be.
Wondering—
Life has meaning,
But not for me.
I don't want to be mean—
Just meaningful.
I wish solitude and quiet,
Peace over everything,
To cuddle me in the void of no person.
My heart is now filled,
With the beauty of ocean colors.
