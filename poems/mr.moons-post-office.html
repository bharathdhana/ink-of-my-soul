Mr. Moon's Post Office

A little girl sat by candlelight,
Writing a letter late one night—
To someone she missed,
To someone above,
A letter filled with sorrow and love.
She raised her head and looked outside,
Through pale clouds and skies so wide.
A full moon glowed, calm and bright,
Bathing her tears in silver light.
Her mother said with a quiet tone,

“If you miss your grandma, you’re not alone.
Write her a letter, from your room—
And send it to Mr. Moon.
He’ll know what to do.”

So the child wrote with trembling pen,
About picnics, lullabies, and “back then.”
Then whispered through the window pane,
Her little prayer, soft like rain—

“Mr. Moon, please send this letter
To my beloved Grandma in the stars.
Tell her I miss her more than ever,
And ask her—
Can she visit in the summer holidays?”

Far away, in the silent deep,
The Moon stirred softly from his sleep.
He heard the voice, pure and small,
And turned to face the Earth’s sweet call.
With a faint smile, he sighed inside,

“My cratered chest is filled with tides—
Of letters forgotten, wishes unsaid,
Words from the living, tears from the dead.”
---
“They ask me for magic,
For love returned,
For moments lost,
For bridges burned.
And though I’m just a ball of stone,
Somehow I make them feel less alone.”

so he cradled her words with care,
Tucked the note in the night’s cool air.
Not promising miracles, nor replies—
But watching her dreams drift into the skies.
